Two Rules

When investing or trading some degree of risk is always involved. However, there are ways to minimize that risk, depending on the type of investment vehicle/style.

There are some general rules like this. Two prominent ones that apply accross the board. These are: Know what you invest in and Don't invest more than what you can afford to lose.

Knowledge

In considering an investment one should do adequate research to understand its basics. Deep knowledge of the inner workings at play is not neccessary, but a basic understanding of how the money is made is enough. Basically, you should do enough research until you are satisfied to take the next step.

Manage Risk

For investments and trading, we can define risk as the amount of funds that could potentially be lost by things going against us in the market. Therefore, it is generally prudent to not put up more money than what you are willing to lose. Don't be in a hurry to bet the farm, because there could be something you are unaware of at some point, like bad news affecting the market you invested in. Though, if you never risk anything you will never enjoy the associated rewards either.

After having done your research and assessing your own finances, decide if things go completely wrong, x is the amount I would be willing to lose. At a later stage one can reasses with increased experience, knowledge and funds.
